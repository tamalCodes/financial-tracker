# Landing page

## Problem

`/` is Kharcha's public marketing page. It turns the existing premium warm product identity into an approachable first impression and guides visitors to `/auth` for signup or login. `/landing` remains a compatibility route for existing links.

## UI behaviour

- Public, responsive page at `/`; no authentication needed.
- Landing navigation and CTAs route to `/auth` for login and `/auth?mode=signup` for signup.
- `sitemap.xml` indexes the canonical public root using `NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_URL` (with a localhost development fallback). `robots.txt` advertises that sitemap and excludes API, auth and dashboard routes from crawling.
- `LandingMotion.tsx` creates Lenis smooth-wheel/anchor scrolling (disabled by `prefers-reduced-motion`) and adds scroll-reactive nav states. Nav sits at page root with the page's highest z-index, so it stays above every hero, card and footer layer. It begins full-width/subtle and compacts after 72px into a restrained 480px × 56px liquid-glass bar. Dark sections declare `data-nav-tone="dark"`; the motion controller detects the section beneath the nav and switches brand, menu and toggle text to cream, returning them to charcoal over light sections. Sticky shell contains the glass header and expanded menu as sibling surfaces, preventing nested `backdrop-filter` roots from disabling menu blur. Both surfaces therefore sample the page independently with adaptive translucent tints and specular inset highlights. Dark expanded glass uses stronger optical diffusion and reduced backdrop brightness so oversized background headings become atmosphere while menu copy remains crisp. A small pointer bridge and close grace keep the menu open while moving from the compact bar into its links; click/tap pins it open, and Escape/link selection closes it. Menu links use the compact Manrope UI voice and gold hover accent rather than display-scale type.
- Hero uses editorial Kharcha messaging, product dashboard-inspired CSS artwork, and a charcoal primary CTA. The `Start tracking` CTA uses a small gold progress rail, an arrow token and a slow specular sheen that passes on its own gentle rhythm (~5.6s interval, ~1.2s sweep) and replays a quicker sweep on hover/focus, suggesting forward motion without leaving the premium Kharcha system. Its preview responds gently to pointer position, graph/bars animate into place, and orbiting money elements create restrained game-like depth. The floating rupee uses compositor-only `translate` motion so it does not trigger per-frame layout. Orbit dots are children of their ring, remain on-axis, and travel behind the dashboard layer rather than crossing its chart. The hero and its closing "quiet place" rail are composed as one combined viewport (`hero` is `100svh - 78px` with the rail nested at its base), so a visitor lands on a single self-contained screen. On desktop the rail is a thin, minimal banner: a small, lightweight caption above a width-capped (720px), edge-masked marquee of compact Spending, Saving, Investing, Bills and Goals pills that crawls automatically (26s linear loop) and pauses on hover, keeping the hero to one screen instead of a full-width static row. On small phones, the same concepts ride a single continuously crawling horizontal rail. Three repeated segments and a compositor-driven linear `translate3d` loop make the track visually infinite and refresh-rate smooth; touch and horizontal-wheel input scroll it natively, pause the crawl during interaction and resume it afterward. Reduced-motion visitors receive the same manual rail without auto-crawl. All other hero motion also stops under `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Sections cover money loop, product proof, a dark-feature contrast section, AI-generated fictional customer portraits, a financial-freedom final CTA and a full-width multi-column footer. Tablet keeps all five money-area markers on one rail and narrows numbered money-loop gutters so indexes, copy and arrows remain aligned. On small phones, each numbered money-loop row places its two-digit index, title and northeast arrow on one shared typographic baseline, with supporting copy beneath the title. The human-proof chapter presents the customer stories as a physical stacked-card deck: `StoryRotator.tsx` renders five fictional members (Meera, Aarav, Riya, Kabir, Ananya) as overlapping cards with the front one in focus and up to two peeking behind at deterministic tilt/scale offsets (deeper cards fade out), giving a premium matte-portrait stack. Each card is a nav-grade liquid-glass surface (translucent warm tint, `blur(24px) saturate(1.3)` backdrop-filter, specular inset highlight) with restrained quote type. The front card is draggable/swipeable by thumb or pointer (release past an 82px threshold advances or rewinds), it still auto-advances every 6.5 seconds, pauses on hover/focus/drag, provides manual progress dots (no textual drag cue), and disables auto-motion and card transitions for reduced-motion visitors. Members without a shipped portrait fall back to a warm initials avatar; only `testimonial-{meera,aarav,riya}.png` exist, so Kabir and Ananya temporarily reuse an existing portrait (their `image` is typed `string | null` so a unique AI portrait can be dropped at `public/landing/testimonial-{kabir,ananya}.png` and wired later). The stories section is sized to `min-height:120vh` (vertically centered content with reduced vertical padding) so large desktops no longer show large empty gutters above and below the chapter; other sections keep their existing rhythm for now. Its three-part product summary sits in a compact, width-capped (720px) centered rail below the headline and story, grounding the chapter in monthly view, bills/EMIs, SIPs and left-in-bank balance without stretching edge-to-edge or crowding the lead copy. Footer separates brand from its link grid: portrait tablets through 1024px show three balanced link groups below the brand; phones switch to a two-column Product/Company grid with Support as one full-width quick-link row, then stack legal copy beneath a hairline.
- The dark product chapter uses two bounded GSAP sheet transitions: it enters as a softly rounded sheet over the preceding cream section, then the existing cream customer-story sheet rises edge-to-edge over its tail. Desktop uses the fullest sheet depth. Phones and tablets through 1024px share a restrained but clearly visible parallax treatment: longer scroll-linked vertical travel, slight scale/perspective settling, and a rounded inset that opens to full width. The follow-up sheet uses translation and a smaller scale settle without clipping, preventing dark gutters around its square boundary. There is no extra handoff chapter, sticky hold, WebGL plane or internal testimonial parallax. The closing dark CTA stays in normal document flow so its boundary remains stable. Reduced-motion visitors receive static layers.
- The final CTA retains its original warm charcoal/gold palette and uses the supplied chrome-hands 3D artwork as the right-side freedom metaphor. The source JPEG remains unchanged and a deterministic alpha-cutout derivative removes only its black matte; a directional horizontal alpha falloff dissolves both source-cropped wrists into the section instead of exposing hard image boundaries. Only the image container drifts subtly, and motion stops under `prefers-reduced-motion`. Phones through 800px and portrait tablets through 1024px place the complete 16:9 artwork in normal flow below the CTA at up to 96% of the available width, with breathing room for drift and no negative horizontal offset; both hands therefore remain visible instead of being clipped. These widths also centre the warm radial glow behind the art and retain near-full artwork opacity.
- The dark product chapter uses generous responsive vertical space and a relaxed headline line height, then delays its card grid, preserving an editorial pause instead of a clustered transition. On phones through 800px and portrait tablets through 1024px, the two desktop feature cards become one compact diagonal composition: monthly view sits upper-left with a slight counter-clockwise tilt, while spending sits lower-right with a slight clockwise tilt and safe edge overlap. Tablet cards use about 84% of the available rail; small-phone cards use 94% so copy stays readable. Their proof demos remain shallow and level within each tilted card. The chapter also shortens its outer padding and grid pause at these widths; landscape tablets and desktop retain the side-by-side composition.
- The landing page is theme-independent from the app: it always renders in the light warm palette regardless of the app's / OS dark preference (the web's theme is separate from the app's). This is enforced by re-declaring the full light `--c-*` token set on the `[data-landing-root]` element (the `<main className={styles.page}>` in `page.tsx`) in `globals.css`; custom properties set directly on that element override the values inherited from `html.dark`. Styling still consumes shared `--c-*` tokens (no parallel palette); those tokens are simply pinned to their light values at the landing root. Dark feature/footer surfaces additionally use fixed charcoal hex to retain their editorial contrast.
- Dark feature demo cards are rotated as whole surfaces: their border, rows, labels and values share the same subtle tilt so the visual perspective remains coherent.
- Footer social row contains X followed by a recognizable Instagram outline icon; no third social placeholder is shown.
- Mobile collapses navigation and section grids without hiding primary actions.

## Design constraints

- Warm cream/charcoal system, sparse gold accents, Fraunces display voice and Manrope UI voice.
- Primary CTA stays `--c-cta`, never gold.
- Product preview is original CSS composition. No copied brand artwork or marketing copy.
- Full AI-agent design guidance lives in `specs/LANDING_DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`; keep it aligned when the
  landing page's visual language, motion grammar, responsive rules, or content voice changes.
